# RFM-Analysis

**RFM Analysis** is a marketing technique used to categorize customers based on their **purchasing behavior**. It helps businesses understand which customers are the most valuable so they can create more effective marketing strategies.

RFM stands for three key metrics:

* **Recency (R):** How recently a customer made a purchase. Customers who have bought from you more recently are more likely to respond to new offers.
* **Frequency (F):** How often a customer makes a purchase. High frequency indicates a loyal, engaged customer.
* **Monetary Value (M):** How much a customer spends. This helps identify your high-value customers who contribute the most to your total revenue.

### In Simple Terms:

Think of RFM Analysis as a way to "label" your customers. Instead of treating every customer the same, you group them based on their habits:

1. **Who are your "Champions"?** (Those who buy often, spend a lot, and just visited).
2. **Who are the "New Customers"?** (Those who just started, so you need to welcome them).
3. **Who are the "At-Risk" customers?** (Those who used to buy a lot but haven't been seen in a long time).

By knowing this, you can send the right message to the right person—for example, giving a special reward to your top spenders, or a "We miss you" discount to those who haven't shopped in a while.
